Source: Hertfordshire County Council SEND Commissioning Award Decision Letter dated 11 August 2025, references HCC2314690 (Lot 1) and HCC2314691 (Lot 2), issued via In-Tend correspondence.

Section 01Case title

This case study examines the route In-Home Carers Limited took onto Hertfordshire County Council's Framework Agreement for the Provision of Homecare and Support Services for Children and Young People in Hertfordshire. The procurement was run by the Council's SEND Commissioning function based at Farnham House, Six Hills Way, Stevenage. Lot 1 carried reference HCC2314690 and Lot 2 carried reference HCC2314691, and the Award Decision Letter reached the provider on 11 August 2025, with the standstill running for ten days and closing at midnight on 21 August 2025.

Section 02Organisation type and service setting

The Contracting Authority is Hertfordshire County Council, an upper-tier authority commissioning children's homecare and support through its SEND Commissioning function. The procurement was undertaken under the Public Contract Regulations 2015 with standard standstill arrangements, and the framework was run from the Council's Farnham House offices in Stevenage with award notifications issued through the In-Tend Correspondence area.

The setting is regulated homecare and support delivered into the family home, foster home or short-break setting for children and young people with disability, complex health needs, autism, learning disability and mental health needs. The service profile spans EHCP-aligned care planning, sensory-aware support, complex health interventions delivered in the home, parent and carer respite through structured short breaks, and trauma-informed support for children with mental health needs.

Section 03Client profile at entry

In-Home Carers Limited is a children's homecare provider operating from Unit A2, Arden Press House, Letchworth Garden City, with a developing presence across Hertfordshire and an ambition to land both lots of the SEND framework. The internal bid function had practical experience but had not yet built a SEND-specific bid practice that read for an EHCP-aligned care planning workflow rather than for an adult-derived template.

SEND Commissioning panels are structurally distinct from adult social care commissioning panels. They read for EHCP-aligned planning, school and SENCo coordination, parent-and-carer respite design and child voice as operational practices rather than as referenced concepts, and adult-derived narrative, however operationally sound, reads as transferable rather than as SEND-fluent on a SEND panel.

Section 04Starting gaps and limitations

Two structural gaps were live at the start. The narrative used adult-derived language for care planning, even though the SEND Commissioning panel was reading for EHCP-aligned planning, school and SENCo coordination and parent-and-carer respite design. Coproduction with children and young people relied on a generic accessible communication paragraph rather than a worked one-page profile structure, with child voice referenced rather than demonstrated, and below those named gaps the safeguarding narrative needed to be anchored to the Hertfordshire Safeguarding Children Partnership pathways rather than referenced through generic safeguarding policy text.

Section 05Trigger for engagement

Hertfordshire was establishing a Framework Agreement for the provision of homecare and support services for children and young people in Hertfordshire across two lots, and the provider needed both lots to maximise eligible call-off opportunities for children and young people across the county and to operate as the credentialled SEND-aligned partner for Hertfordshire commissioning. Single-lot acceptance would have left the provider competing in a smaller subset of the available pipeline.

Section 06Risk position at the outset

The risk was producing two technically eligible submissions that read like generic adult homecare bids with a children's services badge. SEND Commissioning panels filter on EHCP-aligned planning workflow, multi-agency working with schools and SENCos, and parent-and-carer respite design, and without those embedded across both lots the bid would have been evaluated as competent rather than as SEND-fluent. Below that, the standard ten-day standstill following award notification meant any award would be subject to challenge, so the submission had to be defensible on its face by the close of standstill.

Section 07Constraints

The provider was working through two lots in a single submission window, with Public Contract Regulations 2015 standstill of ten days following award notification. CQC and Ofsted regulatory positioning had to be confirmed where applicable, safe recruitment evidence had to be packaged for evaluator consumption, and named operational leads for SEND coordination had to be identified inside the submission rather than referenced in summary. Submission was through the In-Tend Correspondence area, and SEND-specific terminology was required throughout the bid.

Section 08Our role and level of involvement

We took the engagement as lead writer and submission coordinator across both lots. We owned the SEND-aligned method statements, the safeguarding and safe recruitment narrative, the children and young people coproduction evidence, and the lot-specific tailoring required to satisfy the Hertfordshire SEND Commissioning evaluation panel. The provider supplied operational facts: keyworker structures, EHCP-aligned planning workflow, named clinical pathways, parent-and-carer respite design and the safeguarding pathway into Hertfordshire's local authority.

Section 09Stabilisation actions

Three stabilisation actions ran first. The order reflected the SEND-specific nature of the panel's evaluation, in which cohort signalling has to be embedded across the bid before any single answer can carry the weight on its own. Both lots were mapped to the Hertfordshire SEND specification before any prose was drafted, and each method statement was anchored against the Council's exact terminology for the cohort, the setting and the regulatory perimeter, so that where the specification used a particular term, the response used the same term.

The safeguarding narrative was rebuilt around DSL identification, mandatory training, supervision and incident reporting, with named pathways into the Hertfordshire Safeguarding Children Partnership and the Council's Designated Officer (LADO) for any allegation against staff, and the staffing response was rebuilt around safe recruitment, training matrix, supervision, well-being and retention evidence anchored to children's services rather than to adult social care defaults, with references named and right-to-work verification confirmed at appointment.

Section 10Approach to building capability

The bid was treated as a single safeguarding-led submission across two lots, with each method statement anchored against the Hertfordshire SEND specification using named operational examples covering EHCP-aligned care planning, sensory-aware support, complex health interventions delivered in the home, parent and carer respite, and trauma-informed support for children with mental health needs. EHCP-aligned care planning was named as the workflow rather than referenced as a concept, with care plans connecting to EHCP outcomes and reviews running on cycles aligned with Annual Reviews where applicable. Coproduction with children and young people was anchored to a worked one-page profile and accessible communication tools, and multi-agency working was specified at the level of named partner organisations including schools, SENCos, NHS Hertfordshire and West Essex ICB, CAMHS and family advocacy organisations.

Section 11How delivery was executed in practice

The service was described as a family-home-led care offer with named keyworkers, structured handover, EHCP-coordinated reviews, multi-agency information sharing, family-centred communication, and short-break delivery for parent and carer respite. Plain-language one-page profiles supported child voice in care planning, and the narrative held inside SEND territory rather than slipping into adult social care defaults. Care plans connected to EHCP outcomes where applicable, reviews ran on cycles aligned with Annual Reviews, and information sharing with schools and SENCos was embedded as standard practice. Each operational paragraph was anchored to a verifiable system or named role, with safeguarding pathways into the Hertfordshire Safeguarding Children Partnership documented as part of the safeguarding response.

Section 12Key interventions that changed trajectory

Three interventions did the work. The narrative was rebuilt around EHCP-aligned care planning with named coordination roles, which moved the default register of the draft from adult social care to SEND, the dominant evaluator filter on this kind of procurement. Coproduction with children and young people was anchored to a worked one-page profile and accessible communication tools, demonstrated as practice rather than referenced as a concept. Multi-agency working was specified at the level of named partner organisations and named ICB pathways, with the safeguarding route into the Hertfordshire Safeguarding Children Partnership documented in the safeguarding response.

Section 13Outcome achieved

Both Lot 1 (HCC2314690) and Lot 2 (HCC2314691) were provisionally awarded by Hertfordshire County Council on 11 August 2025. The standstill closed at midnight on 21 August 2025 without challenge, and the framework moved into operation thereafter, subject to standard sign-off and compliance checks.

Section 15Before and after position

Before the Engagement

The provider had no place on Hertfordshire County Council's children and young people homecare framework and no structured route to receive SEND Commissioning referrals across the county. The bid narrative was carrying adult social care defaults into a SEND-specific evaluation, and coproduction was being referenced rather than demonstrated.

After the Engagement

Both lots are awarded. The provider operates as an approved supplier on Hertfordshire's children and young people homecare framework, with documented safeguarding and SEND-aligned method statements as the basis for all future call-offs.

Section 16Client confidence and capability post-engagement

The provider now holds an EHCP-aligned care planning template, a SEND-specific safeguarding map and a child-coproduced one-page profile structure that reads correctly to a SEND Commissioning panel. The shift in confidence moved from generalist children's bids to a specific SEND register, which is the dominant evaluator filter on this kind of procurement, and the internal team can now read a SEND specification and identify the cohort signalling, the EHCP alignment expectations and the parent-and-carer respite design before any prose is written. The bid practice has structurally separated SEND submissions from adult and adult-derived children's submissions.

Section 17Client feedback

Award notification, Hertfordshire County Council SEND Commissioning
"Following detailed evaluation by the evaluation panel, I am pleased to inform you that, Hertfordshire County Council proposes to accept your tender together with the other successful tenderers detailed in Appendix 2 - Successful Tenderers in relation to the proposed Framework Agreement. In accordance with the procurement documents, the Council have chosen to implement a standstill period of ten days before it concludes the Framework Agreement."

Section 18Repeat or ongoing work

The framework runs through the contract term. The provider has retained ongoing support for adjacent SEND and children's services procurements across the East of England, where the same EHCP-aligned and parent-and-carer respite architecture applies, and call-off opportunities through the In-Tend system are now part of an ongoing pipeline tracked alongside adjacent submissions in Bedfordshire, Cambridgeshire and Greater London. The bid practice is being applied across new SEND framework windows as they open.

Section 19Similar scenarios this applies to

The pattern recurs across SEND-specific procurements and across children's homecare frameworks where parent-and-carer respite is built into the service profile.

  • Local authority children and young people homecare frameworks.
  • SEND Commissioning procurements with EHCP-aligned care planning expectations.
  • Tenders combining family-home homecare with structured short breaks.
  • Children's frameworks operating with standard ten-day standstill under PCR 2015.
  • Submissions where coproduction with children and young people is evaluated through one-page profiles.
  • Multi-agency frameworks requiring named partnerships with schools, SENCos and ICB pathways.

Section 20Outcome summary

Two-lot framework win on Hertfordshire's children and young people homecare procurement, awarded by SEND Commissioning, with both lots confirmed during a single standstill window without challenge. The provider's bid practice now reads for the cohort the SEND panel evaluates, with EHCP-aligned planning, named multi-agency partnerships and worked coproduction structures embedded as standard practice.
