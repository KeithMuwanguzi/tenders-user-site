# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Directors, operations managers, and bid leads at UK health and social care providers: domiciliary care companies, residential care operators, supported living providers, children's services organisations, NHS-contracted community health services. They are mid-career professionals evaluating whether TenderLab is a firm they can trust with a six-figure contract bid. They arrive having already Googled competitors. They make decisions on behalf of organisations, not themselves. Context: office hours, desktop, considered evaluation mode.

## Product Purpose

TenderLab is a specialist bid writing consultancy for the UK health and social care sector. It exists to help care providers win public sector contracts through expert tender writing, review, coaching, and pipeline support. Success is a care provider signing a retainer or commissioning a bid — not a form submission or a click. The site must do the work of a senior partner pitch meeting.

## Brand Personality

Confident, specialist, human. TenderLab knows more about care sector procurement than anyone else in the room — and it shows without arrogance. Warm enough to reassure a first-time bidder, authoritative enough to earn the trust of a seasoned procurement director.

## Anti-references

- Generic NHS template sites: institutional blue/teal, Arial, government-form layouts, clip art icons.
- SaaS startup landing pages: hero metrics with gradient backgrounds, floating cards with drop shadows, "We empower teams" copy, confetti animations, glassmorphism.
- Corporate law firm dark sites: heavy serif on black, gold accents, oppressive seriousness.

## Design Principles

1. **Authority through restraint.** Every visual choice should signal expertise, not enthusiasm. Whitespace earns trust. Decoration spends it.
2. **The senior partner standard.** Every section should feel like it belongs in a pitch deck from a firm that wins contracts for a living, not a website template.
3. **Sector specificity as differentiator.** Generic looks like everyone else. References to care frameworks, procurement portals, and evaluator mark schemes signal that TenderLab is genuinely of this world.
4. **Human confidence.** The brand is specialist but not cold. Precision in language and layout; warmth in tone and imagery.
5. **Desktop-first credibility.** The primary evaluation journey happens on a work laptop. Mobile must work; desktop must impress.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. No reduced-motion exceptions needed beyond standard `prefers-reduced-motion` support. Colour contrast must be verified at both dark-nav (hero) and light-nav (scrolled) states.
